The State of Crime

In October 2018, President Trump directed the Department of Health and Human Services to declare the opioid crisis a public health emergency.

Drug overdoses are the leading cause of death for Americans under 50.

Overdoses killed more people last year than guns or car accidents, and are doing so at a pace faster than the H.I.V. epidemic at its peak. In 2015, roughly 2 percent of deaths — one in 50 — in the United States were drug-related (NYtimes).

Boston is no stranger to the rapidly escalating epidemic of drug use.

Heroin

Heroin possession is illegal in Boston. The city has the seventh highest death rate from Heroin overdoses (CDC 2016). In recent years, the United States has been rocked by the opioid crisis.

Needles

Boston 311, the city's public service request channel, allows constituents to report used needles lying on the ground in their neighborhood. Used needles are often indications of opioid use.

Marijuana

Although Marijuana was recently legalized in Boston, it is widely regarded as a gateway drug.

Inefficacy of Policies


Boston implemented two policy-changes aimed at reducing heroin abuse in 2016 and legalized marijuana in late 2016. The data shows mixed success of these policies in reducing these drug-related activities at the macro-level.

Bill H.4065

In March 2016, the governor signed Bill H.4056 (An Act relative to substance use, treatment, education and prevention). The act included provisions targeted at reducing opioid abuse, including limitations on the prescribing of opiates, drug-related education, and a Good Samaritan law for those who administer naloxone (an overdose treatment) to someone who is overdosing. For more, see the Massachusetts Medical Society's fact sheet.

AHOPE

Around October 2016, the Boston Public Health Commission's (BPHC) introduced its harm reduction and needle exchange site. Access, Harm Reduction, Overdose Prevention and Education (AHOPE) offers active injection drug users a range of services, including HIV, Hepatitis, and STI testing, anonymous safe needle exchange, and education and counseling. For more, see BPHC's website.

Ballot Question 4 (2016)

In November 2016, Massachusetts voters approved a ballot initiative to legalize marijuana for recreational use. It has taken longer for marijuana stores to open up: just this November the first pot shops opened in Northampton and Leicester.

Let's Prevent Drug-Induced Deaths!


There were 2,061 opioid-related overdose deaths in 2017 (MDPH, 2018). Addiction is a disease, not a crime.

As of October 2018, Gov. Charlie Baker announced that the state Department of Public Health issued a statewide standing order for the distribution of naloxone (brand name Narcan), a life-saving drug that can stop an overdose .

Gov. Charlie Baker also said the state plans to use $36 million in federal funding to combat the opioid crisis and to provide addiction treatment medications to more inmates at county jails, among other initiatives.
